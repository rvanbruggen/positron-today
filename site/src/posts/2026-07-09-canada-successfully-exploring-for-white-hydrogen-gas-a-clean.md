Scientists in Canada have made an exciting discovery—ancient underground rocks are producing hydrogen gas, potentially offering a new clean energy source known as 'white hydrogen'. This resource could power industries and communities while reducing reliance on fossil fuels. Researchers at the University of Toronto found significant hydrogen concentrations in a mine, marking a breakthrough in targeted exploration. This could lead to a domestic energy source that supports local industries and reduces the need for imported fuels.
