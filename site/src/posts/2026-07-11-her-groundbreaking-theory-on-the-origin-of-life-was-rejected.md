Lynn Margulis, a remarkable biologist, faced rejection 15 times for her revolutionary theory of symbiogenesis, which suggests that life evolved through cooperation rather than competition. Her ideas, initially dismissed, eventually reshaped our understanding of the origin of life, highlighting the importance of connection. Interestingly, her work was also influenced by the sexism she encountered in the scientific community. Despite some controversial beliefs later in her career, Margulis' legacy reminds us that science thrives on diverse perspectives.
