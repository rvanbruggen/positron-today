A groundbreaking procedure using high-powered sound waves has successfully treated identical twins suffering from twin-to-twin transfusion syndrome during early pregnancy. This non-invasive method allowed doctors to seal problematic blood vessels without surgery, leading to the healthy birth of the twins, Nancy and Margo. The treatment is seen as a promising alternative to more invasive procedures, and researchers are excited about its potential. As these little girls prepare to start school, their story brings hope to many families facing similar challenges.
