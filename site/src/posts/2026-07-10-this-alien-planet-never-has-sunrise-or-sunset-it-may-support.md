LHS 3844b is a fascinating tidally locked exoplanet that orbits a red dwarf star, presenting one side in constant daylight and the other in eternal darkness. Surprisingly, new research suggests that these extreme conditions might not be as life-threatening as we once thought. The study indicates that tidal locking can help maintain moderate temperatures, potentially allowing life to thrive. It's an exciting revelation that challenges our understanding of habitability in the universe!
