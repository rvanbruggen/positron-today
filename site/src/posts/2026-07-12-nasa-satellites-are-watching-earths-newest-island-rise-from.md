It's fascinating to think that NASA satellites are capturing the birth of a new island in the Bismarck Sea! On May 8, 2026, an underwater volcanic eruption was detected, and since then, researchers have been closely monitoring the activity. The images show plumes of ash and discolored water, suggesting that something big is happening beneath the waves. This is a rare opportunity for scientists to witness and study such geological events in real-time.
