In Nigeria, women’s conservation collectives are stepping up to protect the critically endangered gorillas and their habitat. These inspiring groups are not just patrolling but also creating rules to combat environmental threats like river pollution. It’s fascinating to see how these women, feeling let down by men in their communities, are taking charge and making a real difference. Their efforts are sparking similar initiatives in surrounding villages, showing that grassroots movements can lead to significant change.
