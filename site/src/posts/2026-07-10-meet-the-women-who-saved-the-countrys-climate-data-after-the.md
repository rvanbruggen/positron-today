This inspiring story highlights the incredible efforts of Rebecca Lindsey and her team to save crucial climate data after it was taken offline. When the Trump administration shut down NOAA's Climate.gov, Lindsey and her sister Mary, along with Anna Eshelman, stepped up to create Climate.us, preserving over 15 years of vital information. They recognized the need for reliable, science-backed climate communication, especially as political influences threatened public access to critical data. It's a powerful reminder of how passionate individuals can make a difference in the face of adversity.
