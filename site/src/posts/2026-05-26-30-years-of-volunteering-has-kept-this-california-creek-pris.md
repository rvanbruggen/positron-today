It's amazing to see how 30 years of dedication from volunteers has transformed a small creek in Oakland into a vibrant ecosystem! The Friends of Sausal Creek have worked tirelessly to restore this important waterway, ensuring it supports native species like rainbow trout and the endangered pallid manzanita. Their efforts include planting tens of thousands of native plants and monitoring wildlife health to keep the creek thriving. This story is a beautiful reminder of how community action can lead to extraordinary environmental restoration.
