Isn't it fascinating that pigeons have a special way to navigate using their livers? Recent research reveals that immune cells in their livers, called macrophages, are sensitive to Earth's magnetic field, acting like an internal compass. When pigeons fly, these cells align with the magnetic field, helping them find their way. This discovery not only sheds light on animal navigation but also opens new avenues in understanding the immune system's capabilities.
