Lung cancer is the leading cause of cancer deaths globally, but researchers are optimistic about a new pill that could prevent it. This exciting development comes from the emerging field of cancer interception, which links inflammation to cancer risk. A simple blood test combined with anti-inflammatory drugs might help identify those at risk and stop lung cancer before it starts. It's a fascinating time in cancer research, and the potential impact on public health could be enormous.
