So, convincing someone who disagrees with you can be a real challenge, right? Dr. Alison Wood Brooks from Harvard Business School suggests that the key is to avoid invalidating their views. Instead of jumping in with 'I disagree,' she recommends creating an emotionally safe dialogue where you show genuine interest in their perspective. This approach allows for a more constructive conversation over time, making it easier to change minds without the immediate pressure of disagreement.
