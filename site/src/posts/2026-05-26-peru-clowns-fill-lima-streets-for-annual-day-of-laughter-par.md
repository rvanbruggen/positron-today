Peru's annual Day of Laughter brought joy to Lima on May 25, with hundreds of clowns parading through the historic center. Dressed in vibrant costumes and oversized shoes, these performers turned the Plaza de Armas into a festive spectacle. The event honors the legacy of José Álvarez Vélez, affectionately known as Tony Perejil, who dedicated his life to spreading laughter. Despite lacking official recognition, the gathering showcased the spirit and humor of Peru, with messages like 'More clowns, fewer politicians' adding a playful twist to the celebration.
