Scientists have discovered a new forecasting technique that can predict the strength of the Sun's next activity cycle up to seven years in advance. This method focuses on a newly identified 'switch-off' stage in the solar cycle, where severe space weather ends abruptly. Initial estimates suggest that Solar Cycle 26 could be moderate, comparable to the current cycle. Researchers expect to refine these predictions in about two years when they can base their forecasts on direct observations.
