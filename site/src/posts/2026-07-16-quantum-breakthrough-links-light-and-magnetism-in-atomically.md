Researchers at the City College of New York are making exciting strides in quantum science with atomically thin materials that connect light, charge, and magnetism. This groundbreaking work reveals how excitons, created by light, can interact with magnetic states, paving the way for advanced optoelectronic devices. The findings suggest that these materials could lead to new technologies that manipulate light and magnetism together. It's a thrilling time for quantum research, as scientists explore the potential of these unique interactions.
