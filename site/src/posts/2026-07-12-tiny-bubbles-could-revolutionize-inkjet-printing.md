Researchers at Tokyo Metropolitan University have discovered a fascinating method to control ink drying by incorporating ultra fine bubbles into ink droplets. This innovative approach allows for remarkable changes in the drying pattern, which is crucial for manufacturing tiny electronic devices. Unlike traditional chemical additives, these bubbles disappear completely, leaving no residue behind. This could lead to cleaner and more precise printing techniques for advanced microelectronics.
