So here's the thing - Lindau hosted what you could really call a 'Davos for science', bringing together Nobel Prize winners with the next generation of brilliant young researchers. I find it fascinating how these gatherings create space for the most accomplished scientists to mentor and exchange ideas with people who are just starting their journey in research. The five-day event had this wonderful energy where experience and fresh thinking collided in the best possible way. And what strikes me most is how vital these face-to-face moments are - you can't replicate that kind of intellectual cross-pollination online.
