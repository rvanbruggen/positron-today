Isn't it fascinating? New research shows that ancient Egyptian princesses were not just symbols of royalty but skilled archers! Scans of their mummies revealed strong muscle attachments and healed fractures, indicating they actively used the weapons buried with them. This challenges the long-held belief that these items were merely symbolic. It turns out, these royal women were engaged in physically demanding activities like archery and hunting, showcasing their strength and capabilities.
