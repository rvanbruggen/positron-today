I've distilled the entire Positron idea into a single-page poster - think of it like the Holstee manifesto, but for good news. The core message is simple: the news isn't the world, bad news is just louder, and our brains are wired to fear what's dark while overlooking what's bright. But good things are happening everywhere right now, real and ordinary - they're just quiet, and they deserve to be seen. This isn't about pretending the bad stuff doesn't exist; it's about balance, about reading the good alongside the bad and remembering that hope is rooted in real physics, just like the positron itself.
