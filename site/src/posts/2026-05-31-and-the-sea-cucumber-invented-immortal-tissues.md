A Canadian team has made a fascinating discovery about sea cucumbers, showing that fragments of these creatures can survive and even grow for years in seawater. This is quite remarkable, especially since it's the first time such small parts of an animal have been observed to remain alive and thrive. The researchers stumbled upon this during a routine inspection of an aquarium, and they were initially skeptical about their findings. It's a thrilling glimpse into the potential for life and regeneration in the ocean!
