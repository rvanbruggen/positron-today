So, Dr. Arthur Brooks, a happiness researcher, emphasizes that true joy in the second half of life comes from focusing on internal well-being rather than chasing endless desires. He suggests that as we age, we should shift from wanting more to wanting less, which can lead to greater satisfaction. By redefining success and practicing gratitude, we can nurture meaningful relationships and find deeper purpose. This perspective is a refreshing reminder that happiness is often about appreciating what we already have.
