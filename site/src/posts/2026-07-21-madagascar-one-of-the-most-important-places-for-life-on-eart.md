Madagascar is at a pivotal moment, with a chance to lead global conservation efforts and protect its unique biodiversity. The island is home to over 150,000 species that exist nowhere else, including its iconic lemurs. Recent political stability has reignited conservation initiatives, with a commitment to protect 30% of land and sea by 2030. Local communities are stepping up, proving that conservation and prosperity can go hand in hand, making this an exciting time for Madagascar's ecological future.
