Scientists at Johns Hopkins Medicine have made an exciting discovery using lab-grown brain tissues to predict how Alzheimer's patients might respond to treatments. These organoids, which are small clusters of brain cells, could lead to more personalized care for those affected by this condition. The research also revealed that these mini brains release particles that may serve as new biomarkers for diagnosing Alzheimer’s. It's a significant step forward in understanding this complex disease and tailoring treatments to individual needs.
