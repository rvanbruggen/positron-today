I've been digging through 91 days of data from 37 news sources, and it turns out that feeling you get on Sunday - that the news is just heavier - is completely real. The weekday average sits at about 7.4 percent clearly-positive articles, while weekends drop to just 4.8 percent, with Sunday hitting under 5 percent. The reason? Newsrooms work office hours - all the good stuff like science stories and feature pieces come out Monday to Friday, while hard news and conflict keep happening 24/7. So the weekend gets thinner newsrooms, less of the uplifting content to balance the scales, and the mix curdles. It's not you dragging yourself down on Sunday - it's the calendar.
