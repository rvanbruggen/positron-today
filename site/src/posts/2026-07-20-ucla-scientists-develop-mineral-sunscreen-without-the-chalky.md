UCLA scientists have made a breakthrough in mineral sunscreen by creating a formula that minimizes the chalky white residue that often deters daily use. By reshaping zinc oxide into unique tetrapod structures, they've managed to enhance UV protection while making it more appealing for various skin tones. This innovation could significantly encourage more consistent sunscreen application, especially among those with darker skin, who are at higher risk for late-stage skin cancer. It's exciting to think that a simple change in appearance could lead to better skin health for so many people!
