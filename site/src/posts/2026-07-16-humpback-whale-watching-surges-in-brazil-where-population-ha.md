It's amazing to see how the humpback whale population in Brazil has skyrocketed from just 2,000 in 1986 to around 35,000 today! This surge has made whale watching in Guanabara Bay a popular activity, especially during the migration season from June to November. Tourists are thrilled to catch glimpses of these magnificent creatures, and local experts are optimistic about their recovery. It's a beautiful reminder of how conservation efforts can lead to thriving wildlife and a boost in eco-tourism.
