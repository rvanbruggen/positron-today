Isn't it amazing that the Andean leaf-eared mouse can thrive at altitudes over 22,000 feet? These little creatures have developed unique adaptations to survive in such extreme conditions, where oxygen is scarce and temperatures are freezing. Researchers have discovered that they can produce heat efficiently and even metabolize desert plants that would be toxic to other animals. This fascinating study not only sheds light on these remarkable mice but also offers insights into how other species, including humans, might adapt to low-oxygen environments.
