Southern right whales have a fascinating behavior of floating upside down, and recent drone footage reveals it might be a way for mothers to take a break from parenting. These moms lead intense lives, migrating long distances while caring for their young without eating for months. The footage showed that mothers spend a significant amount of time resting, and some even flip upside down, which could help them manage energy and feeding times. It's a unique adaptation that highlights the challenges these incredible creatures face in their natural habitat.
