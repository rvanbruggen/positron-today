Scientists have discovered a fascinating bacterium from Japanese tree frogs that can eliminate cancer tumors in mice with just one dose. This groundbreaking research shows that instead of just altering gut bacteria, we can use living bacteria to directly target tumors. The bacterium Ewingella americana not only attacks cancer cells but also boosts the immune response, making it a double threat to tumors. While these results are limited to mice, they open up exciting possibilities for new cancer therapies in the future.
