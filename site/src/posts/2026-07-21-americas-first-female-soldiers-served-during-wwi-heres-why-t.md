During World War I, the U.S. Army needed skilled telephone operators, leading to the recruitment of women who were fluent in English and French. These pioneering women became known as the 'Hello Girls' for their exceptional communication skills. Despite their significant contributions, they were initially classified as civilian employees, not military personnel. It took over 60 years for them to gain recognition as veterans, highlighting their incredible perseverance and dedication.
