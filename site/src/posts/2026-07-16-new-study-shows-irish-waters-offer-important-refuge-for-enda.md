A new study reveals that Irish waters are a crucial habitat for the endangered basking shark, the second-largest shark species. Drawing on nearly 50 years of sightings, researchers found that these gentle giants return to specific hotspots each year, particularly during spring and summer. It's exciting to see that the number of sightings and group sizes have actually increased over time! This research highlights the importance of public participation in conservation efforts, showing how community involvement can lead to positive outcomes for endangered species.
