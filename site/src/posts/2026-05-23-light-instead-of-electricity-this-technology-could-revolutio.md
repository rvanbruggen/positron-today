Researchers at the University of Pennsylvania are exploring a groundbreaking idea for the future of computers by using light instead of electrons to power AI systems. This shift could overcome the physical and energy limitations faced by traditional electronics. By creating hybrid particles called exciton-polaritons, they aim to combine the speed of light with stronger interactions typical of matter. This innovative approach could lead to significant energy savings and more efficient computing for AI applications.
