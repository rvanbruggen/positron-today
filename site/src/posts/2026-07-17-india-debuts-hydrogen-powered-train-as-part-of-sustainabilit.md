India has just launched its first hydrogen-powered train, marking a significant step in its sustainability efforts. Prime Minister Modi inaugurated this zero-emission locomotive, which will operate on a 90km route in Haryana. This train, named the 'NaMo Green Rail', is designed and built in India, producing only heat and water vapor during operation. It's exciting to see India joining the ranks of countries like Japan and Germany in adopting this innovative technology!
