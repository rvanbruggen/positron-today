Isn't it fascinating that some insects can thrive under extreme water pressure? Lake fly larvae, specifically Chaoborus edulis, can dive over 1,200 feet deep in Lake Malawi, using unique air sacs to control their buoyancy. Researchers discovered that these sacs can withstand pressures far beyond what was previously thought possible for insects. This discovery not only challenges our understanding of insect capabilities but also opens up exciting possibilities for material science with the resilient substance found in their air sacs.
