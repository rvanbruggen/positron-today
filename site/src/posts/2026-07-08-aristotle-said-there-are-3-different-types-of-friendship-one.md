So, Aristotle really had some fascinating ideas about friendship! He categorized friendships into three types: utility, pleasure, and the good. Friendships of utility are more transactional, while those of pleasure are based on fun and enjoyment. But the most valuable, according to him, are friendships of the good, where both friends share similar virtues and genuinely wish well for each other. It's a reminder that the best friendships are those that help us grow into better people.
