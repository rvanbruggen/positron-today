It's fantastic to hear that levels of harmful 'forever chemicals' in seabird eggs have dropped by 70% over the last 55 years! This remarkable change is linked to regulatory actions taken by North American governments, which is a testament to the power of environmental oversight. The study focused on northern gannets on Bonaventure Island, revealing significant decreases in chemicals like PFOS and PFOA. This is a clear win for both wildlife and public health, showing that regulations can lead to real improvements in our ecosystems.
