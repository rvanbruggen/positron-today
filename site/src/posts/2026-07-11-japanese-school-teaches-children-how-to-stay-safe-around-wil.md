At Ina West Elementary School in Japan, kids are learning how to stay safe from wild bears through a unique bear awareness program. They get to touch real bear fur and see skulls while experts explain bear behavior and why they sometimes wander into towns. It's a crucial lesson, especially since bear sightings are common in the area. This initiative is a proactive approach to ensure the safety of children and the community, reflecting the reality of living alongside these magnificent creatures.
