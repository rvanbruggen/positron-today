At Soul Fire Farm in New York, Black women are reconnecting with their ancestral farming practices through immersive programs. Participants learn about the deep historical ties between Black women and land, which date back to pre-colonial times. Leah Penniman emphasizes the importance of healing relationships with the land, especially after the painful legacy of slavery. This initiative not only cultivates food but also nurtures a sense of community and empowerment among those involved.
