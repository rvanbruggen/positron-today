This summer, Europe is facing devastating wildfires, and in Spain, nearly 100,000 hectares have already been consumed. A unique initiative by the 'Tivissa Donkey Firefighters' association is using rescued donkeys to help combat these fires. These donkeys eat the most flammable grasses, acting as natural firebreaks while also receiving care and a chance to reintegrate into a herd. It's a win-win situation that highlights the special role these animals play in fire prevention.
