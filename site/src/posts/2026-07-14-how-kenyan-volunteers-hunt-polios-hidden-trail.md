In northern Kenya, volunteers like Eroi Lemarkat are on the front lines of the fight against polio, racing against time to investigate reports of children with sudden paralysis. With no recorded cases since 2013, the challenge lies in detecting vaccine-derived strains in under-immunized communities. These dedicated health workers travel deep into remote areas, often relying on local trust to collect crucial stool samples. Their efforts are vital in preventing outbreaks and ensuring that no child is left vulnerable to this disease.
