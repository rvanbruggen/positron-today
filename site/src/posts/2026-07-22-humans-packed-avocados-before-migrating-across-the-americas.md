About 5,000 years ago, humans took avocados on their journey as they migrated north from South America to Nicaragua. They planted these seeds in Central America, leading to a fascinating hybridization with local varieties. This resulted in a diverse range of avocados that are still enjoyed today, although they differ genetically from the ones we find in stores. It's amazing to think how these delicious fruits have such a rich history tied to human movement and agriculture.
