So, have you ever seen a bird that looks like a quirky mix of a heron and a cartoon character? The boat-billed heron is just that, with its unique flat bill perfect for catching fish and crustaceans. These fascinating birds are mostly solitary but become monogamous during the breeding season, raising their young with care. While their population is decreasing, they are still classified as a species of least concern, which is a relief compared to some other heron species out there.
