Scientists in Germany have made a nasal vaccine that can shut down a Covid-19 infection in the nose and throat, achieving 'sterilizing immunity' and preventing illness. This approach could make our respiratory infections less of a threat with future vaccines boosting mucosal immunity. The US struggles to contain the virus as hundreds die daily, but researchers hope next-generation vaccines can help. A new vaccine takes a weakened form of the virus that is no longer a threat and then shows it to the immune system. This technique is called codon pair deoptimization
