Antibiotic resistance is a significant challenge for modern medicine, but researchers are finding innovative ways to combat it. A team at Cold Spring Harbor Laboratory has revived the effectiveness of vancomycin, a crucial antibiotic, by using a small molecule that blocks a bacterial enzyme. This exciting discovery shows that existing drugs can be reactivated rather than creating new ones from scratch. By sharing their molecular library, the researchers hope to inspire further breakthroughs against drug-resistant infections.
