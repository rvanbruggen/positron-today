So, it turns out that some monkeys might have a better grasp of geometry than we thought! A recent study shows that olive baboons and Rhesus macaques can understand geometric relationships similarly to humans. This research suggests that our ability to comprehend shapes and space could be deeply rooted in our evolutionary history. How fascinating is it that these primates, along with children and adults, think about geometry in fundamentally similar ways?
